## 📌 Overview
This level focuses on **advanced logical reasoning** and **non-obvious condition handling**.  
Problems here are designed to stretch thinking beyond straightforward checks and simulate **interview-style logical traps**.

**Phase:** 1 – Conditional Thinking  
**Level:** 5 – Creative / Tricky Logical Scenarios  
**Language:** Java

---

## 🧠 Concepts Covered
- Multi-condition logical evaluation
- Mathematical pattern detection
- Time and date reasoning
- Digit-based computation
- Edge case handling
- Logical optimization without loops

---

## ✅ Problems Solved

1. Determine whether a point lies on:
   - X-axis  
   - Y-axis  
   - Origin
2. Check whether three numbers form a **Pythagorean triplet**
3. Validate a **calendar date** (day & month, leap years ignored)
4. Calculate the **smaller angle** between hour and minute hands of a clock
5. Check whether three numbers form an **arithmetic progression (AP)**
6. Check whether three numbers form a **geometric progression (GP)**
7. Check whether, in a 3-digit number,  
   **(first digit + last digit) = middle digit**
8. For an integer (1–9999), check if:
   **sum of digits > product of digits**
9. Compare two dates (day & month) and determine which comes first
10. Determine the **century** for a given year  
    (e.g., *19th century*, *20th century*)
